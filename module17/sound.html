The Music of Video Games

While video games are notorious for being fun to play, music and sound in video games is a very important aspect of the medium. A game with great gameplay can be hurt by a weak soundtrack. A great soundtrack has a huge effect on a player's experience with a game. A powerful score is the best way to have people remember a game for a long time, even beyond its initial life. To stand the test of time and be revered as one of the greats, a video game must include good music and sound design.

There is something extremely enriching about video game music, I can't help but love it. Almost all of my favorite songs actually come from video games, not record labels or popular singers and the like. Good video game music is very easy to memorize and that then allows me to become familiar with a game in a way that makes me feel at peace. Whenever I work I almost always put on some video game music to help me relax and focus. There are little things in this world that are better than the nostalgic tunes of a game I fell in love with during my childhood.

And although a good soundtrack helps to create a much better video game experience in of itself, it is important to not forget about Sound Design. While controlling a character it is vital that the character's footsteps have sound to them, and the area you are in within the game must translate to different frequencies of sound. For example, when you control a character underwater the sound should become muffled as it would in real life. If the player is in a cave, it would make sense for the cave to add an echo effect to certain audio cues. This logical method of designing sound around a player's activity creates a more interesting and dynamic experience, even if it may be subtle towards the player.
