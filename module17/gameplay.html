Favorite Games

Team Fortress 2: An online FPS with 9 distinct classes for a variety of gameplay options. This game has a cartoony artstyle and features an insane amount of hats to wear. It's basically a war themed hat simulator. The 9 classes come from different places around the world and each has their own unique and quirky personality traits which translates into a charming and hilarious experience. And yet, still the core gameplay is worthy of being under the title of timeless masterpiece. TF2 is mostly known by its iconic Rocket Jump feature. The Soldier is a class in Team Fortess 2 who uses a rocket launcher as his primary weapon. Aiming at the ground and shooting after a well-timed jump will allow the soldier to blast jump very far distances both vertically and horizontally at the cost of some health. This gameplay mechanic creates variety a of high-level exchanges with enemy combatants and opens up a large skill ceiling for long-time players.

Xenoblade Chronicles: An epic JRPG spanning across the backs of two massive titans. The story has some crazy twists that had my mind struggling to come to terms with what just occured. The gameplay contains a unique hybrid of real-time action and turn based combat which requires key positioning in order to get the most out of certain attacks. For example, the Back Slash attack does 100% more damage when hitting an enemy from behind. It is impossible to not love the characters and their charming British banter. The music is simply divine and the sense of scale felt by the wonder of discovering bizarre lands on such a gigantic beast is truly magnificent to behold.

The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask: A legendary Action Adventure game following the tale of a young boy named Link who must save the land before a possessed Moon is able to crash into the ground in 3 days time. This game features a time travel mechanic which allows the player to go back in time as many times as needed in order to prevent the disaster that looms overhead. But when time is turned back, progress for many actions in the world and story reset while key items are retained. The world and characters around Link are affected by the time of day, and thus the player can manipulate time to create certain outcomes in their favor. Combat revolves around the use of a sword and shield alongside a surplus of support items such as a bow and arrow, bombs, and a hookshot. A big aspect of this game are the different masks which can be obtained as the player progresses. Each mask has a unique effect on Link ranging from total transformations to invisibility against enemies.

Dark Souls 3: Part of what is widely considered one of the most difficult video game franchises of all time, Dark Souls 3 is the most advanced and exciting of the 3 games. The game takes place in a midieval Lovecraftian-esque setting where the world has been desolated by the undead. As a hollow yourself, you continue to ressurect after each death and seek to reignite your being with the embers of fire. The darkness and isolation felt while playing this gruesome and difficult game focused on visceral and thrilling combat is quite overwhelming. This game will have you die over and over and it won't pull punches. One of the biggest parts of this game are the epic boss battles that will leave you blown away by the bombastic orchestrated music and brutal fights to be had.

Super Smash Brothers: This entry isn't particularly one game, but the whole series. Nothing in this world brings me more hype than Super Smash Brothers. The appeal of this fighting game is definitely the huge roster of iconic video game characters which spans a massive lineage of popular and cult classics across the ages of time. There's something strangely enticing about Pac-Man beating Mario to the ground. New character reveals and speculation for said characters is one of the most fun things to think about while waiting for each new game in the series. There's actually a new one coming out for the Nintendo Switch this year, and E3 is going to be revealing it for the first time ever. Thinking about these games can seriously burn me out because of the insane amounts of hype it generates. Smash Brothers is different from any other fighter game out there because it requires you to damage the opponent enough to knock them off the stage in order to take a life from them. Instead of HP (Hit Point/Health Point) bars, the characters rack up damage based on percentage values. The higher the value, the farther they get launched when taking damage.

Games at their Core

Surely, storytelling has been heavily innovated on due to the interactive elements that video games have introduced to the entertainment industry. Controlling a character within a game can make you feel immersed in an unknown world like never before. But storytelling is not all that video games have to offer. The main meat of entertainment that can be found within the world of video games is the fun aspect of simply playing them. Gameplay is a huge part of what makes a game, well, a game. And the evolution of that gameplay has been staggering over the past few decades compared to what the oldest games used to be like back when Pong was considered new to people.

There are many gameplay mechanics that create fun and exciting play sessions for people of all ages to enjoy. Video games have limitless potential since they are not bound by the physical limitations of our world. It is up to the developers to use their creative vision in order to make a special and entertaining video game. If you can think of it, it's probably possible in a video game.

A big part of what makes a video game feel good to play is the frame rate. The ideal frame rate is a solid 60FPS (frames per second) since this displays 60 frames on a screen per second. Games at 60FPS look fluid and deliver a more enjoyable gameplay experience. Some old games ran at pretty low frame rates around the 20FPS mark, and going back to play them today kind of hurts. It is a bit hard to play a game with 20FPS since it is noticably choppier than a game running at 60FPS. While a solid 60FPS is ideal, a solid 30FPS looks great too. It is more important that a games keeps a consistent frame rate than a high one. If a video game has an unstable frame rate, then the visuals on the screen will feel laggy and can easily rip people away from the experience.

Many games are focused on combat, or at least have some sort of combat element. Often a game will pit the player against enemy AI (Artificial Intelligence) of increasing difficulty as the game progresses. Being able to combo enemies into several different moves for devastating finishers is one of the most satisfying things when the combat feels fluid and engaging. Upgrade systems that improve your character's ability in battle make for a very rewarding feeling of progression. Feeling like you are becoming more powerful in a game while the enemies are also increasing their potency just feels great.

While many single-player games include AI combatants for the player to compete against, there are also a lot of games that utilize the feature of PvP (Player versus Player) gameplay and combat. Video games are fun alone, but it's even better to grab a friend and play a cooperative game which requires teamwork in order to succeed. If teamwork doesn't suit you though, you can always have a friendly competition in 1v1 battles. If one on one isn't enough, why not take it online and play against players around the world?

Online games introduce the concept of engaging with people in video game matches over the Internet. It's great fun to crush an opponent who you know is actually another player somewhere out there struggling to figure out how to play the game. Finding someone who is on your skill level is also cool because they can provide you a challenge which AI opponents can't. Human players can usually think up many more options to beat you in a game compared to a pre-programmed AI.

Combat is my personal favorite form of gameplay in most games, but there are many other types of gameplay depending on the game you're playing. Some games may have the player solve puzzles and instead have a focus on exploration rather than combat. Another popular type of gameplay is racing. But even if the main goal of a racer is to get to the finish line first, certain games such as Mario Kart by Nintendo include items that allow the racers to grab items and fling them at other racers to hurt their chances of winning. These items make for a chaotic and hectic race that is more exciting than just driving around a course normally.

Another popular form of gameplay involves platforming where the character jumps around to collect certain key items in order to progress to new levels and unlock secret areas. By far the most popular and one of the best 3D platformers ever made is Super Mario 64 by Nintendo.

Games that focus on story might give the player a difficult set of choices which can drastically alter what happens to the characters and world around them depending on what they do. Telltale's games come to mind when thinking of this type of choice driven interactivity is involved. Two of my personal favorites of theirs are Telltale's The Walking Dead and Telltale's The Wolf Among Us. Both of them have engaging and atmospheric narratives that fuel the need to find out what happens next.

The First Video Game

Entering the first game ever created: Tennis for Two. This game was created by William Higinbotham in 1958. Tennis for Two was not too different from the more well-known arcade game Pong which was released in 1972. The only objective of Tennis for Two was to hit a ball towards another player's goal to try and score. The controls consisted of a single knob which turned the angle at which the ball would be shot, and a button which shot the ball. As long as a player attempted to hit the ball while it was in their court, it would be hit back towards the other player.

While Higinbotham's video game may seem ridiculously trivial and basic by today's standards, this game was a revolutionary concept which had never been thought of until then. William's game did not pick up much attention, so he left the project to the wayside. Ironically, the creator of the first video game had no idea how big of a deal video games would later become, and he had no interest in them despite that. He was a physicist after all, and he only incidentally created a video game because he wanted to appeal to people touring his lab by providing them an interactive experience.

The First Icon in Gaming

The earliest commercial video games had very little gameplay elements and restricted control options due to technological limitations. Computers themselves were rather young at the time video games were first being created for them, so not much could be done when designing a game with a weak system holding the developers back.

One of the earliest video games to achieve popularity with people around the world was the arcade game Pong, developed by Atari. Although this game was similar to the first game ever made, it had more gameplay features that made it a complete video game. For one, it actually kept track of each players' score. Players could control a paddle on either side of the screen to hit a ball back and forth as it perpetually moved across the court. The paddle could only be moved vertically and depending on the angle the ball was hit at, the ball could be hit diagonally at increased speeds to confuse the other player. The goal of the game was to score 11 points before the other player in order to win. Pong was the first commercially successful video game and paved the way for the video game industry as a whole.
